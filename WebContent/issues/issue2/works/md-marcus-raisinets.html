she lost her Xanax somewhere between here
and her sister's funeral in Tennessee
the long bus ride home claimed her medication
and she's back in this place too soon

explanations glide unobstructed
over polished gums of holes in hands
plucked during three sleepless nights

darkness swallows babblings
blood runs opaquely
aloneness surges in black tides
if a tree falls in a forest
and no one is around to hear it
does it?

the pharmacy won't give
her anymore of those pills she hates
the home she lives in hates it
the taxi should have never
left because this won't take much longer

a smile given with a box of Raisinets
her last, sacrificially offered
gift to my children
who play
oblivious to her dilemma
with toys meant
for much younger children
she would like me to call the taxi driver
for her so she can go back to the home
she hates

a receptionist shouts out
making her turn her head, giving me
enough time to check the plastic
shielding the candy box
because she hasn't had her Xanax in 3 days
