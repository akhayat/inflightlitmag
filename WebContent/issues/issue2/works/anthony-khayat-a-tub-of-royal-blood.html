For my dearest love
and newest drug—
Skyrim.

It's one o'clock
and all I got
are my boxers
on, and you got your
succulent suction cups
all wrapped up
around my eyes,
and in the meantime,
my dirty dishes
are stacked to the sky
and beer number five
is all dried up,
joining the Goose Island
graveyard on my
makeshift coaster—
an unpaid speeding ticket.

They call it fantasy,
and for me
it's easy to see
why.
It's not just
the fire breathing,
the sneaky thieving,
the spellweaving—
the real magic
is the mouse clicking.

Click,
you're a master blacksmith.
No back sweat needed.

Click,
you're a warrior,
a conqueror,
your sword
turns obstacles
to corpses.

Click,
you're a sharpshooter,
hawk-eyed archer.
No practice,
just clicking.
Clicking makes perfect.

Click,
you're alive again,
quicker than Jesus.
No white lights,
no Reaper,
no risk,
all reward,
just wait for it to load.

Click,
you're a king.
Here's your crown
and your castle
and your syringe
pump,
pump,
pumping
a tub
of royal blood
into those
dusty,
common
veins of yours.

All you need
to do is
click.
