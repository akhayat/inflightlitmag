I went out at near-dusk, walking,
Welcoming the bear,
Welcoming the bear that had torn me open
The year before.

I went out into gathering darkness,
Fearless,
For what had I to fear now, after seeing for myself
Heaven,
Hell,
And all distortions thereof,
After seeing and surviving them all?
And yet wanting only what eluded me,
Or nothing at all.

I set out, welcoming and wishing for the bear,
Fearing not death,
For what a relief that would be;
Fearing not the meeting, face to face, eye to eye,
Of human and ursidae;
Fearing not the chaos,
Nor the surrender,
Of ruby-red release.

Of liquid garnets,
In rivulets.

I had feared the bear before,
But not that evening,
No, and it would have caught me by surprise,
For my sight was obstructed by twilight and
Hard tears, like a hard rain.

Had I but met that bear again,
And I did not, that darkening evening,
(and thought this a shame),
I'd have bared my white throat for it,
Exposed my heart to it,
Where the marks of its claws were already made.

I would have loved to let it finish
What it had started.
