This isn't what you think
it's going to be. The sea,
muttering some empty
promise to the beach,

is reaching for us.
Speaking of which...
Isn't it time we unhitch
our flaws from the whipping post

so they too can get
the most out of life?
Or are we yet too selfish?
My dying wish has finally

kicked it, sticking
to the side of my pocket
like loose change, and now,
the careful arrangement

of my pride is threatened.
You, my love, have been
to me a gracious foil,
not because we're so

starkly different, but
because we're exactly alike.
Don't argue with the facts.
Lately, we're so at home

with what we lack
that our first act
has lost its gravity.
It's habit we cultivate,

based on an unstated
hatred of novel ideas.
Let's admit this finally.
Let's admit ourselves finally

into life's vast sanitarium,
don the white gowns
of mediocrity,
and allow ourselves sleep.
