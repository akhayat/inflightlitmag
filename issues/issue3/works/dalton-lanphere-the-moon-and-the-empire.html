She held the face close to her, feeling the tent growing inside her. Saccharine waves caressed her and the trapeze waved. The face was apish and rude on her breast. She sat for a moment on the dirt floor. The face suckled at her limbs.

The barking stars locked in with the spotlights. The moon hung low overhead. They gnashed their teeth at it. The moon was lost in a radiator*. The heat made it sickly and the sheets vanished from underneath. Falling.

She felt her hands slipping from the bar. The moon was high now and the stars only growled. The interim moments screeched through her head. The warm dirt floor held her close.

The moon shattered around the barking stars and the tide only flowed. Echoes cut and drums erupted in the grove and moved through the lemon trees.

She pulled her hand out from underneath and the red choked. The apish, rude face abated.

A gentle breeze crawled against the night.



*In reference to the lady in the radiator from the film Eraserhead. “In heaven, everything is fine.”
Lynch, David, Jack Nance, Charlotte Stewart, Allen Joseph, Frederick Elmes, Jeanne Bates, Judith A. Roberts, Laurel Near, and V Phipps-Wilson. Eraserhead. United States: Absurda, 2002.
