your bedroom was on the top floor. a large room in a huge house overlooking the park. you had a view to botany bay. i would dream  of waking up to a view of the water, of watching planes taking off & landing. of lying awake at night next to you & watching the glow from the refinery.

we danced around each other for months, flirting, not knowing where it would lead. going off with others, feeling almost jealous.

new year’s eve on the roof. your latest lover making a fool of himself – trying to pick a fight. he threw a punch but missed & stumbled then slipped. he slid down the gentle slope of the roof, laughing drunkenly as he disappeared off the edge. we heard him land on the second floor – he had only dropped one floor. he got up and waved before climbing through the bathroom window. twenty minutes later i saw him walking off down the street.

six months later another party – a big winter party with illegal fireworks in the park. a group of us staggered out in the dark with a box of, skyrockets, catherine wheels, roman candles. for fifteen minutes we lit wicks, ran and laughed and screamed as coloured lights chased us around the park. dancing back into the party we found the hall full of water. somebody said that a sewer pipe had broken – but the water looked clean to me.

eventually i fell asleep drunk on your bedroom floor while you lit candles & fucked peter in your bed. when i woke he had  gone & you had placed a pillow under my head. in the morning we had breakfast together.
