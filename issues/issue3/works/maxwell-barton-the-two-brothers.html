The two brothers sat in front of me on a black leather couch
in the basement of our friend’s townhouse
on Friday.
American Beauty played quietly on the turn table beside them.
A cloud of smoke filled and settled out the room.

James sat statuesque on the edge of the couch
blowing smoke rings in my direction.
His posture was that of an equestrian;
his features—defined and rigid;
his brown plaid shirt sewn with the thread of imperturbable logic.
He radiated an honest wisdom,
and his eyes, darting and snarling, fed from the essence of the room.

His brother, Colton, beside him,
relaxed his body into the bosom of the cushions.
His back was rounded like a Christmas wreath;
His features caricatured—soft and round;
his skin, illuminated under the yellow light, shone like a Basque sunset.
He brought a presence of light-hearted artistry to the room
and with each breath, absorbed the inspiration of our surroundings deep into his lungs.

The two brothers sat in front of me on a black leather couch
in the basement of our friend’s townhouse
on Friday.
They sat shoulder to shoulder;
laughing about the trivialities of life;
enjoying The Grateful Dead.
