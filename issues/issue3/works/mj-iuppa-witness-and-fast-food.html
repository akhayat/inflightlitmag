She’s mopping the red tile floor, mopping bits of french fries
& hamburger, splashes of milkshake with the poise of
ballerina & weight lifter        swing dancer & garbage man
synchronized swimmer & G.I.    The swish of her stringy mop
misses corners, and under high chairs.  She’s lost in the works,
lost in the crime of country music complaining, “I didn’t see you
standing there,” and he is, suddenly, behind her.  She turns, smiles
slowly reads his shirt “you suck” out loud.  He stammers,
“I didn’t see you.”

First appeared in Mockingbird. (print journal).
