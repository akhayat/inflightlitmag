You’re so Romantic, you said, me hoping you meant that more than admiringly or kindly,
    more than as a comment on my general sensibility.
But I could never tell, with your everlasting diplomacy and your sugar-rush smile,
    whether anything you said was with kindness, or with a kiss,
or with champagne-dry shyness and no thought to how truly sweet I thought you were.

And I couldn’t tell at all whether you were merely sensitive to touch and scent,
    or wanted me to catch the subtler sense. I had never met anyone who loved books
like you loved books, but who hadn’t made that loving their life’s work.
    And yet you sat with me, in the student cafeteria, handing me apples
and shuddering as you put your nose to the mist of flapping pages.

Have you ever really smelled things? you asked me. Just put your nose up to this apple. It’s,
    ah…they’re all so different, you know…so sensual (So sexual?).
Or at least I saw the sex inside the apples as you held them up and shuddered
    and asked me to share that shudder, too. But I wouldn’t ask if you wanted to share
more with me than the scent of Grimes Golden or those dusty, thin French books.

Did you sit, sighing, on a log over a spring near some bridge north of Hanoi,
    those summers when your parents left the city’s young flourished chatter,
hoping for a stay against your staying in America to take your dose of international medication,
    and did you read French romances, hoping for a boy with silver eyes
to play the flute for you at festival? Or maybe that was only me, not knowing how

I could pre-imagine you, walking into the auditorium, for, of all things, a poetry reading
    (which I had arranged, and till you arrived, thought nobody would attend),
who, years before, had lain lazy and lonely near calm water, hoping for your shining eyes
    to come and speak to me. To me, you were the first woman from the future world,
the day after history.

And yet I failed somewhere; somewhere, the trace stops. Was it when you bumped into me
    smiling, as if tracing your fingers down my forearm softly: please,
and could you please, be a little more Romantic? Or was it when you said you couldn’t
    come to meet me, but when I went there for other reasons
I found you holding a newspaper over your face (and oh how I thought that you hated me then)?

Or was it when I put the interview with you in the paper because you were so
    refreshingly interesting (oh please don’t think I meant that nasty word exotic),
and we became what could only become friends, then fade away? Or did I imagine
    every electric signal; were you ever even waiting for the move I didn’t make?
I can only hope that if you find this, that you would forgive me. That this isn’t just another mistake.
