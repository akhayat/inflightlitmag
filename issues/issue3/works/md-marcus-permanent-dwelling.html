salivating with regret
memories collecting on the tip of my tongue
working themselves on the wrong side
of gravity, sliding back down my throat
choking the air that would have vibrated
into something beautiful,
like a song
or at least into something useful,
like your name

this longing is too old or too thin
and I’m much too heavy-handed
to cradle it with enough finesse

this dried leaf caught in a wind chime
whose crude crackling of disintegration
is drowned out by metallic clanging
meant to illicit images of heavenly hosts
the translucent flutter of fairy wings
nothing whatsoever to do
with a gradual dying

without a tangible hand or hope to hold
on to, I remain no more
than some whirligig caught
in a tempestuous tornado of what-if’s

no more than the loud hollow
click of a tight key turning the lock
to an empty house
