Always clip your toenails.
Be sure to do it often
And to cut them round.
Because cracked toenails hurt
And in-growns are such a pain on pointe.
If your pointe shoe fitting was less than an hour
You’ve been gypped $115 with a pair that’s a full size too big everywhere.
Be sure to keep medical tape and fabric tape handy,
It’s hard to dance with raw, bleeding blisters.
And don’t put lotion on your feet.
For the rest of your dancing career,
The more dry skin the better.
Be sure to smile every time it hurts,
It’s a good way to make sure you’re always smiling.
Be sure to point your foot, and to turn out with the hip
Rather than your knees or ankles.
Be sure to put some extra wear on those tendons,
All in the name of flexibility.
And to put your doctor in a tailspin,
Whom you’ll be seeing very often.
When beginning pointe just remember two things:
1.) Pull up out of the shoe so your toes stand straight.
2.) Beauty is painful, and pain is not psychological despite what your instructor might think.
Be sure to memorize every plot, composer, and song of every ballet you ever dance to.
Learn Italian, French, and plenty of Russian.
(Ballet moves are in all of these languages)
Actually, go ahead and learn all the European languages while you’re at it.
Dance has been to a lot of places.
Be sure to stretch
That way, your chance of being able to walk the next day is still slim
But it’s better than nothing.
Buy lots of dancewear
Not for class,
But to show off your pride at school.
Your leg warmers and leotard are your jersey.
But don’t wear the leotard to school,
Everyone will be able to see the back of your bra.
Be brave.
Take pride in who you are,
Because today’s society is going to test you
And tease you.
But you must make the show go on.
Keep your chin up, show confidence,
And don’t let haters get to you.
Keep your posture and composure,
They’ve never experienced pain like you have.
A pulled tendon is worse than a broken bone.
And don’t let dance’s history influence you,
Show-biz has always been rough, dirty, and mean, but you aren’t.
Keep your ears high,
Strut that perfect posture ‘til all are envious.
Take pride little Ballerina,
Or Ballerino,
If you are a boy
Which does not make you flamboyant,
But rather a bird.
You fly higher and react quicker than any football player could ever dream
You give off expression in a way that few can master,
Making you a masterpiece.
Stay strong.
It’ll help your form.
Always look up
You’ll twirl longer.
Don’t forget to smile
Even though it hurts.
And above all,
Don’t forget to clip your toenails.
