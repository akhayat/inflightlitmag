I’m sure that if Einstein had had access to Facebook he too would have randomly looked up pictures of his ex and then became overwhelmed by the close smallness of the new world (his musings on the sticks and stones of the fourth world war, notwithstanding)
She’s married now and looking happier than ever, I was the last gasp before submission to something bigger than either one of us would ever want to be, she called it Mormonism and I called it adulthood: nice going Einstein
This is not a rational world, not one bit
And how many times have I gone over, even as I had lain next to countless other women (ok it was 4) that night we spent skateboarding around the University of New Mexico’s campus occasionally holding hands while we rumbled over pavement?
That is countless, because the internet allows your wound to stay fresh; allows her to be a click away as long as I am plugged in I will never be free
It’s not that I still want her, I’m beyond that, it’s just that I miss those few soft days in the light (and make no mistake, I’m quite aware this how a number of dictators got their starts too)
Relativity allows some people to enter adulthood smoothly and some to burn up in the atmosphere so that a half decade later, some of us are still hot to the touch: untouchable in fact
