Alone of all nights this is the loneliest,
with every flower gestating in the mast.
Even the witch hazel is waiting to pucker
and chafe in the impossible cold. Even
the Christmas lights, the window candles
of richer neighbors in Georgian revivals,
even the red poinsettias and their givers,

Rich chocolates and rich blue paper
have done nothing and cause me to crash;
it is January 7th in my soul. Even
old Christmas is over; Jamestown
is burning, and this is not Brasilia. In a dream
I pass through the green filaments of Tennessee,
remembering warm water and blighted pine
above the dams of the river Ocoee. Once
I was with others there and needless, alive
with the smell of wet earth and white ash;
now the one is us who loves mushrooms
and the smell of rain has been betrayed,
and obligation keeps me from lighting out
with this boy with his compass who screams
like a mandrake root every time I am called
to the office of Platonic ministries.
