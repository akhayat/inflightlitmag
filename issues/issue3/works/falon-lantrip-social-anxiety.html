Shallow shifts in
consciousness, I admit
that I am afraid.
Time will tell what it
has always told, that I am
too old to feel this way.
Ticking and tocking,
nervously rocking,
cracking knuckles,
my confidence
buckles, I admit
that I am afraid,
and that I am
too old to feel this way.
*
Energy spent on
a nervous twitch,
on thoughts which
are out of my control.
If only I could be
a ghost, but I am
a host of a parasitic leech,
draining me of my
self-esteem. You’ll say,
because they all say, I am
too old to feel this way.
So, I put on my best face,
and face the world head-on,
but, I admit
that I am afraid.
*
I’ve always been just a few
puzzle pieces short of
seeing the bigger picture,
because I’ve always cut corners,
and I’ve always
been awkward.
“Your skin will get thicker,”
my mother would say;
“Sticks and stones,”
but those fragile, fleeting
tones flying off of tongues
like knives into my soul
are what have always
hurt me the most. I am
too old to feel this way,
and I admit
that I am afraid.
*
Tenaciously intuitive,
tediously diluted,
I am terribly ill-suited,
though I always dress
for the occasion.
My acute awareness
to equilateral situations,
these strangers and I are
juxtaposed. They are
not like Me.
They do not understand
Me. They are so at
ease. Who am I to impose
my silent hawk-eye evaluations
of their clothes? How they compare
to my clothes?
Am I out of my element
again? Do they even know?
Do they even notice?
*
I
am
too
old
to
feel
so
alone.
