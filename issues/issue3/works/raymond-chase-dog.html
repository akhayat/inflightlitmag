You keep injuring yourself.   
I promised you the deer would not invade,  
But you chase them in your sleep  
and bark to warn us.  

3 a.m., dark and cold outside.  
This house does not hold the heat.  
You stumble on the steps,  
Breathe smoke, wince, but hold the line.  

Walk the perimeter, piss and  
Return to assure me we're clear.  
But we're not.  
I wish I could explain to you why we're not clear.  

You know. I can see your eyes.   
This house does not hold the heat.  
You know your valiant efforts are vain.  
It's not the deer who are coming.  

Sleep and dream of dogs you've met.  
Songs they've sung back to you from across the ravine.   
Does that scatter before your charge.
Fences that defeat the impulse to run.
