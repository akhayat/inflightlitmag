She sat there with her small intestine splayed
across the dark mahogany table, a small blade
saddled gently in the nook of her index finger,
fastened firmly into place by her thumb.
Her other hand drew careful circles
over the mushy, writhing mass of organs.
In the distance, she could hear the tap
dripping conversationally to an empty basin.
The scalpel rattled gently on the table.
Gurgling protestations rumbled through
the bloody pile as her hands impatiently
palpated the pinkish, lumpy lengths.
Following only intuition, she searched,
waiting for that telling feeling deep in her gut.
