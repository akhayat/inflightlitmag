We learned to converse in lengthy
phrases of pulsating silence, shaky
breaths offering the words that we
persistently muted with noisy gulps.

The sun wandered in, out of place
amidst pillars of black and rivers
of dripping saltwater pearls; she
flitted through the crowd, lost.

Feet shuffled and the hallway
emptied hollowly; the house
breathed little; relief remained
an abstract and distant mirage.

Feeling dues paid, the sun
departed, trailing a wake of
red and blood orange as she
disappeared over the horizon.

The assault of warm colors
dissipated, leaving cool streams
of blue and grey to shadow walls
and faces, obscuring the bruises
seated on baited tongues.

I tasted only potent venom as they
nestled immovably by my tonsils:
small, incommunicable spheres
exacerbated with every swallow.

Burrowing beneath mountains
of blankets, I hack as they catch
my panicked breaths and collide
against my nightmare-infused pillowcase.
