This poem is called, “I used to eat my feelings.”

I used to be 5'4 and 235lbs, I was so round you could paint a map on me and call me a globe. Kids would roll me downhill and reenact Temple of Doom; I was the boulder.

“Fear of heights.”

I can't breathe on cliffs and high buildings, cause I know I'll try and step, not to fall but to fly. I want to grow wings and watch people. Like an angel without the death part.

“I talk about death a lot.”

I used to want to die, I don't now. Throw me off a building and people will catch, there are people between the barrel and my temple.

“The end.” 

I have a family now, handpicked and cherished like rose gardens, yes some are pricks, but the blood that bleeds finally isn't mine, it's ours, we don't beat to the same drum but the same heart. Shout out to all my friends, my added family, my real, true family.
