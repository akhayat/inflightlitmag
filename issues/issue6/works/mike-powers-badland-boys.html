he has summer on his lips,
aura sun plastering dry outlines of Badlands clay hand prints
on pale chest - just below collar bone-
shirtless and breathless
running game of water-like illusion,
love in the heat of mid-July.

shower cloud above thunders booming God-voice,
heard only on deaf ears
as droplets fell around us in sweet serenity,
washing away the red marks of adventure
and blaspheme.


shelf of rock cascades downward in silent, thousand year remedy
to boredom's lack of vivaciousness.

he parades about the top stone
like some Cherokee forefather,
armed with fox bone staff
and branch of non-native invasive killer's finest
green plumage, to speech and leech of my sinner skin.


he had set about his ruler's gaze
across the land,
declaring nudity and blaring it excessively,
peach bits exposed to the wayward, noon sun--
for we were the last men on earth
in that moment,
cradled together in the yellow mud
and silver crust
of earth's battered face.
