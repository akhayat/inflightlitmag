I had been following a winding path through the woods for hours when I heard his TV playing in another room. The voices sounded frantic. He had been watching the news throughout the day with his mother in hopes of seeing the world end. It was the most important thing and almost a secret. I said, “Hey, man, you all right?” He said “Hi,” but that was it. An ominous crowd of birds perched on power lines. Leaves fluttered down. The streets named for trees were being renamed with numbers. I was unusually touched by the gesture as I had just recently seen a demonstration of robots versus kung fu. By the time I got back, autumn was trending on Facebook. Some called it suicide. Others called it justice. It was shaped like a combat boot.
