Monsters sit on trashy couches
Pink cockroaches climb up
Those crumbling walls
Dogs mate in the corner
And rats eat a chicken carcass on the floor
The swine lay around
Leaving puddles of oil in their seats
All of them scatter however,
When I decide to bare my teeth

You can bleach
And you can scrub
And you can clean and polish these walls
And make it so the shell
Shines eerily in the white
But the organs of this place are dying
And will soon seep through the cracks

Ironically,
I have to wash you from time to time
And rid you of color
Because that reminds you of the black
And me of the horrors in your head.
Though I can make these notes invisible
I can never seem to clean your nerves
But they soon become dirty again

So we sit on the floor together
Alone in pools of filth, madness,
and a desperate hope for white.
I can’t tell if it will ever come.
Besides your greatest efforts
I’ll still hold your hands
They’re slippery from the ink
But I’ve done all I can

We try so hard not to look into their eyes
Even as the claws run down your back
I can only wince for you, even if I look forward.
And when they run down mine
Please don’t scream so loudly dear
You’re going to make my ears bleed

And finally we leave
The walls crumble
The glass shatters
The dogs neutered and the rats spiked
The cockroaches squished against the floorboards
And the pigs cooked alone.
That oily place burns behind us
As I hold you hand and sharpen my teeth.
The monsters follow us out on rusty bicycles.
“You should have killed them”
You say with a kiss.
