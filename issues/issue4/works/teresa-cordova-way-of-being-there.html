The only way of being there for you is
sitting on the opposite side of the couch
with a cup of coffee. You notice my breathing
it is not heavy but
it is good enough to know we are alive.
The crevices around the yellow walls—
they are stars and
there’s a noose hanging from the ceiling that has been exciting you
until today when you smiled for the first time in a
long time. You place a hand on your face,
your beard—it’s turned into sandpaper.

Smile is a foreign language you speak
like a baby looking in a mirror.
He doesn’t know what is looking back at him
But he smiles anyway.

I looked over at you a couple of times
and each glance was a prayer, of redemption
written by my brown eyes
for you
to rise again—
it is almost here
the holy, sacred, second coming.
