For the bird, the dip towards the mouse is everything.
For us, there’s a dot dipping out in the crevice between hills.
For the mountain, how long has it been waiting for its soft purple stubble?
For us, the world still looks like winter and we’re begging it.
For the leaf, this is the wrinkled wailing morning of its life.
For us, this is just dawn and the sun gets in your eyes. It's time for work.
For the sun, the sun is not in a state of constant rage--it is itself.
For us, this is the first day of the world if we think of it that way.
For the world, we are a small distant occurrence.
For us, small distant occurrences are the entire world.
