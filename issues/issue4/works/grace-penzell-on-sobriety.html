I have wasted hours
marking
  windows
       of sobriety,
cherishing them like precious
stained glass,
worthy of reverence
like you would show
inside a cathedral.

but behind the proud,
soft velvet
heavy
curtains of memory,
the windows are
small,
often murky fogged and
in truth,
almost always held together with
        tacky clear packing tape.

when broken, they shatter,
and boards replace glass,
and inside,
    mood lighting replaces the sun,
and the
light bulbs cast shadows
that feel like a home,
the dim uncanny
forms spaces I can’t clearly see
so I don’t have to
    open my eyes and
        take a good look.
because in there
smoke clings to clothing and hair, and
in there
stale beer soaks through the carpet, and
    in there,
    in the past,
when nose blood and vomit
painted the walls,
the bulbs would sometimes
burn out into
absolute black,
and there’s no point straining
to see through that.

it’s true that replacement panes
are now much less expensive,
and mornings aren’t
greeted with the
white lines and pretense, but the
windows,
            still,
are never fully clean, and
seven years later
I’m still fucked up,
and the lights still burn out into
        absolute black, where I
bruise myself on
can’t remember,
and wonder if maybe please sometime
            soon,
I’ll stop making windows and
            tear down walls,
then learn how not to be
            afraid of the light.
