And I hate hate hate it
when my voice catches on your name like a loose thread
of a jumper
catching on a rusty nail
and I just
miss when
your name
didn’t make me unravel so

A squall of conversations unfinished
because you left me trembling in your wake
grasping at driftwood, wondering if a person could ever
willingly start a tempest.
Because it wasn’t always like this
and although I know it’s there
I cannot pinpoint the moment
we left our love out in the rain
and let it rust

I am left
with the collar and elbows of the grey jumper you made me
I still have the label on the inside that
stutstutstutters your initials.
They say that the quiet before a storm is near silent.
I say,
the quiet after a storm
is much quieter.
