Eventually you stop trying to fit in

And go for what fits  

Mr. Salesman I’ll take the dirt-tint, ashy-sidewalk warrior, everywhere-mans,

At discount

All stitched up like an assembly-line Frankenstein  

Laced with ridicule and stigmas  

Red tagged like sin  

And I’ll wear them out if no one minds

Across waves of desolation and long stretches of immaturity  

Until we’re bare and wrinkled from the holes that had worn through

While communing with the grass, and the sidewalk, and the asphalt

Imbedding nubile flesh into all the crevices and folds, this good land has to offer

Melding rawhide with the soul in faded fads you never intended to shine in

Taxing scuffs that leave personality forming scars  

O’ if you could measure wisdom in the unit of human feet

Only then would we understand what it is to walk a mile

Tattered and ragged and blotched and salty and decrepit  

Sojourn so long the soft suede of youth becomes the leather of seniority   

To the point when people stop asking about where you got them

And ask why you still wear them

Long back home, where they beckon charity from loved ones

Strong convictions that it’s time to lay ‘em down

In a box somewhere, in the corner of another box somewhere

So ya’ break down and buy in

Intoxicated, new-born smell  

And when they hand you the box

You can’t help but realize,

Before you wear them in,  

You got to wear them out.
