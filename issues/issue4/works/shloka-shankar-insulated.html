Words have drilled holes in my body.  
Eternally.
A cadaverous instinct to give up haunts me.
I push myself into the cave of another day,
wind milling in the dark – the switch perpet
ually broken.

Disclaimer: None of this is profound. It's voluntary.

I battle against a mind that has been
insulated against change, giving birth
to my own rut and revelling in it.

A sense of anger underscores
the cells in my body, redefining
what I do, and how I do it.

People living in glasshouses
spray paint hallmark-card-greetings
into the void and pretend they care –
shields as powerless as I am.

You know what I know?
I'm left with a tray full
of half-baked truisms, and
images as stale as morning breath.
