noun; 1. an irresistible compulsion to return home; intense homesickness



The mountain was a thorny issue

that must be talked around, all the way,

before it could be touched upon,

headed up a one-car road where death

and courtesy were knit up

like twin brothers.


On one side, the scrub slopes of Kite Hill,

the desolate panorama of downtown

and the railroad yard,

the fox-tail weeds where I found my

first Hustler with the cover torn off,

that first pink, first

unfathomable

object that I hadn’t yet learned to desire.


On the other side, viral bougainvillea,

the suicide gradient of Eldred Street

snuffing out our vision of bikes.

Instead, we hid in the canyons between

our houses,

sneaked through and paired up,

drugged on cold cola and Pop Rocks –

a make-out session with artillery.


Game Boys will be Game Boys.

We were so close

to accidentally seeing

our reflections in the screens,

though I guess we never did.


Here, it’s hidden:

our magnum opus memory, opalescent

descent into the most tired folds of the hillside

at sundown.


It’s an insane valuing, an ice cream truck chime

that only I can hear.

I want

to be found some afternoon,

for my image to be called forth

by a set of power-chorded strings,

a sparked memory, a song-salve

that staves off chest-pains

in the midst of shacks becoming McMansions,

the kickball field and the Eagle’s Nest

being razed to build a media center,

and epic Sunday afternoons boiling down

to just another name

you can’t quite remember.
