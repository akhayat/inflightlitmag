“You’re young, you have your whole life ahead of you, and you can do something with your life.”  He mumbled, staring at the popcorn ceiling. I couldn’t look at him either. The conversation was too stale. I knew then that was the last time we would ever be together.
“It’s just hard—”
“Don’t” He turned on his side to face me. I loved how his eyes were always so worn. Like they had seen so much, but still managed to form a perfect shade of glass blue; the only piece of him that wasn’t distorted by age. The only true innocence that was left of him.  
    “Just start over,” he enforced as his hand warmed my thigh. I let out a deep breath.
    “Stop.” I always hated when he said that.  
I tilted my head towards him, “Alright”
Whether he meant it or not I can’t think that way. He had too many ulterior motives to send me away, and my conclusion has never been that he wanted what was best for me. He was too afraid to come out and say he was over what we had and he wanted me to leave him.
I hear people talk about how they wish they would have known it would be the last time they would share a moment with someone. Like they would have done something different.
    For me it was more of a loss. I knew it was the end and I froze. I tried to stop the end from coming and didn’t enjoy what was occurring. It was the loss of fluid movement and connection. There was no energy in the way we handled each other. People fall apart for numerous different reasons. I’m just glad I will never be the asshole that beats around the bush.
