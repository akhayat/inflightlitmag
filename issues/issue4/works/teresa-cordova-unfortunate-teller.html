Sí se puede, tienes que creer.
My dead grandmother whispers to me.
It is hard to hear her in the clouds,
over the screams.
They shoot like bullets
from one mouth to the other
then into my body. Pouring out blood-like
is the putrid smell of answers,
the truth of the impermanence of love.

Fire red and sadness blue colored
sirens,
to follow the collisions of minds that
do not know forgiveness.
Two men in uniform come and ask the questions
we ask ourselves. How did we get here?

Miscommunication is merciless in the
way it travels through space
and takes people into the unknown,
the darkness of black holes.

Being able to read minds would solve
all the problems in the world.
The evanescence of all divorces
and of the lovers who wear a thick
coat of fear
every season.
No more tears—like bad weather.
The cul-de-sac of prior broken hearts,
escapable.



1. “Sí se puede, tienes que creer” Spanish for “Yes you can, you have to believe”
