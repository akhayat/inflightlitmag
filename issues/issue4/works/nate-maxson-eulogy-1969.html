Schrodinger’s rose is not a bomb but an unauthorized program detected, train rattling down the tracks just like we used to imagine them/ calling sister midnight, you’re wanted in the operating theater

It’s 1969 ok/ War across the USA… amusingly those who ought to remember that particular song, don’t though to be fair we’re about as far from it in 2014 as that year was from the Civil War/ the transience of Gettysburg t-shirts sold in bulk to the tourists (with plenty representing the losing side too!) ought to be a comfort

So let’s talk about time for a moment shall we?

Time can be cut into and segmented like smoke or a fine cheese whose smell is enough to drive away the uninitiated/ cut like a film, cut like butterflies preserved on pins: I slice it endlessly but the only difference is that I’m wholly aware of how many people I’ve had the chance to be in my short attempt at syndication

Have you ever noticed that, for example: on the BBC, a vast amount of the television programs seem to take place in the 19th century?

There is a reason for this: that century is when the British believe themselves to have been at their best, their peak so they subtly regress into a Dickensian afterlife but perhaps a bit cleaner (there are after all, standards to maintain)

Now us Americans, I think we’re slowly doing the same thing: sliding into the past, a few months back there was a documentary on Woodstock that my parents were very excited about and I felt nostalgic too because I remember when I saw a documentary on Woodstock as a boy almost twenty years ago

Of course, it isn’t just television where this parlor trick manifests, that’s just a symptom of a future we refuse to bear witness to, even as it comes down on us like a cloud of smoke and signals we slide slowly into the past

And sometimes we dream forests, great forests with no sky: we vanish into their thickets and inside, why: all the birds sing songs by The Beatles in the morning but like the physical rainforest it’s shrinking, the 20th
century is our event horizon

And after?

After all the yippies and the punks and the greatest generations and thousand art forms from our very brief Athens fade into the past, grow dim between the trees and you no longer recognize the songs, what then?

Then,

Then: we’ve done this before, Nietzsche called it the eternal return and the first cycle of the industrial epoch (hallowed be the name, p-tooey) ended with a white light in Japan

So we’re going to make the machine, make the flesh, flesh the engine and escape into dreams, into an endless Woodstock Wonder Year and never come out until we’ve firmly stopped time with its wheels and all the world will sound like classic rock radio and maybe it won’t be so bad: a comfortable “vast wasteland” which is a term used to describe television by the chairman of the FCC in the same year of our lord 1961 that Eisenhower left office and created the similar prophecy against “the military industrial complex”, make of that what you will

It happened before: the machine made flesh, this was the goal the trick of it all and unconscious, I once dreamed about a young Harry Houdini standing atop a paper-mache mountain on a stage looking pale and rubbing his stomach nervously dressed in Wagnerian lederhosen

A flaw in the film, overheating and a line of dust in your vision must be nothing but that: dust, dust on your century (on my century really too) faster than anyone can blow
