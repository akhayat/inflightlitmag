Robin Lucas is a writer, poet and humorist, and a frequent #HashtagWhore on Twitter. He lives in the Inland Empire of Southern California, an area notorious for including San Bernardino. Its other notable attractions include the highest concentration of meth labs in the state, the worst particulate smog in the country, and the third most hyper-inflated housing market in American history.* The author calls it the “Inbred Empire,” and assumes the credit for coining that phrase.


He began writing at 22, mostly by accident, for the same reasons many writers began writing by accident: 1) he had a typewriter, 2) he had plenty of time thanks to being underemployed, and 3) he had a sudden urge to express his festering self-indulgence (third-person bios, for example). He continues to write for similar reasons, except #1 because his typewriter was eventually stolen—probably by an irritated roommate tired of hearing the keys tapping at all hours of the night. He published his first poem at 26, and this will mark his first publication anywhere since then. He blogs at dryhumpingparnassus.com.


* These “statistics” haven't been verified.
