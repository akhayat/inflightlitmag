I reminisce and I remember about the time
when I would listen to the rhythm
of poetic brilliance, before accepting the title of poet.
I would sit beside the ghosts
of Charles Olson, Ezra Pound, William Carlos Williams
and Allen Ginsberg, and together,
we would constantly critique, between sighs and laughs,
the creation of others, amidst the appreciation of the audience.
We would contemplate the potential corrections
that could enrich the echoes of internal emotion,
tied to the function and the metre of the line,
in order to better perform the deliverance of words
written out of our consciousness.
But, having now perhaps poisoned the future
of my endeavour's outcomes, by branding my material
with the unanimous packaging of poetic licence,
much like the works I used to contemplate,
I have become a target for critical, academic observants,
keen to sink their new found teeth
into the material of imaginative woe.
Little do they know about the challenges faced
by the ink I use, and now that those memories have been erased,
I must become an armadillo, to fend myself
from the ever constant complaints
regarding how my work is not contemporary.
Besides, what is the definition of that word?
As I struggle to find an answer,
I look behind me, as if expecting to see
my younger self, perhaps even in black and white,
to accentuate the ancientness of the unwanted warning
which falls from between my teeth, forbidding me
from taking the classes of multiple professors,
who dared to desecrate my lines with such villainy.
Clearly it never worked. (Pause for dramatic effect).
