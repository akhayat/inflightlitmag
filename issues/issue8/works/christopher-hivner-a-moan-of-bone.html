The horns blew with righteous indignation,
an emergency flare
set off in a crowd
that couldn’t handle the funk,
couldn’t understand the rhythm,
found their bodies moving
against their will.
“We don’t do that here,”
they cried,
a plea the horn players
ignored with
a staccato burst of
trumpet and sax
and to make sure the world
was paying attention,
a moan of slide trombone.
