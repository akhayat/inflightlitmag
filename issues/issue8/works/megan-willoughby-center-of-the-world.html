for Scott

i remember you at the summit,
your shadow reaching

like an unanswered prayer
toward the valley below.

i didn’t know you were uprooting,
didn’t know you were dipping toes

into the bright, raucous vacuum
of the unknown. i didn’t know

you went there to hide,
to die, the small animal of

your body hungry for silence.

***

i never returned to the place
they found you—that hushed mountain

you loved—Liyikshup, meaning perfect balance
but for me, meaning crack in the sky.

***

balance—the world full
of you. the rift—
before you left
this place, trees once grew.
now they backbend,
collapse into the hollow.



Note: Liyikshup is a Chumash term meaning “Center of the World.” The term is attributed to their appreciation of Iwihinmu (Mt. Pinos), which they believed was a center of cosmic balance.
