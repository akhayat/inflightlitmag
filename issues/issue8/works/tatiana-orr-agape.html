Your skin golden brown, as if the sun lie in my arms

Oh, that you would stay there evermore.



That I could behold your eyes

Sparkling in the darkest of nights,



As your arms cover me with warmth

From fire ignited beneath your breast



Never to be extinguished;

Even in your passing it does not cease to burn



Tears yet fall onto feathered pillow

With memories of you whispered in my ear



Each day a fleeting moment revived

Felt in the depths of my spirit



Solace finds itself as the firmament parts for you.

Gentle kiss remains on my lips



That I may recall the taste of you with each passing breath

Eternally.
