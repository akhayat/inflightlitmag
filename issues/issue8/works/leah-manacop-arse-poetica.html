Even the bruises I left on your arm last night are deafening [1]

I can only muffle them with kisses [2], breathe

through your mouth [3] so I can only smell the living

spurts [4] that shudder, two-weeks writhing,

on our sheets [5] -- why don’t they

fade? Do tongues [6] laced with longing taste more

of the eternal [7]?


In touching each other, you have

caressed the truth [8] of me [9].
