First and most easily it's ocean

that'll allow one to understand how

the universe in its physicality

is so coldly indifferent and therefore perfect:

if a being's well-being is

to the detriment of another—

indifferent, physical frame—Fine!

"Life is a festival only to the wise!"

Joyful participation in the sorrows of the world!

To see impending mountains of sea is to know!

 

And God's some secret thing that permeates this foam,

from the searing white burn of star,

to verdant moss in a summer's creek—

ninety-nine-point-nine percent space:

a place of peace beyond any duality—

how could Saint Augustine wonder whether a

boulder, dolphin, boy, or sequoia held more god?

 

Along with creation, the window through

which Time comes pouring in,

and the question of corruption

within transformation and opinion—

lions corrupt gazelles;

an old plow left by a homesteader

in search of gold further up river

will alter with weather, where perhaps

many years from now, or tomorrow

a wanderer will find it covered in grass

and guess how old it is.

 

The grand continuum,

hierarchy of Being from mind to matter—

existence with a capital E is good! And

the closer to pure mind, the greater goodness is;

while matter pulls the soul down to discourse

of doing laundry and party invitations;

and external things that exploit any

weakness in the will—

how does a great angel become the Devil?!

A creature of God created out of what was

created—did He leave an element in

He could not transcend, or do we

thank the Devil for being able to blame?

 

Is it close to classic?

Along the lines of the ruse

Hermes put over on Apollo,

where Zeus lets Mercury be elusive:

the silver-chrome bead, whole and inviting,

allowing one or two to bear false witness.

 

Of that which is conscious and that which is Un,

unconscious evil would only be Yin:

hopefully only a wrong done within

the innocence and fear of childhood;

every other is where free will mixes in, and

your evil is to remain uninformed.

 

If it's truly Yin, it will be done—

but you can close your door, whereupon

virtue's born; and times no open door's found,

last shot shut with a bang,

black disc Yin flips to Yang.

 

And so, of the dark in the chiaroscuro

of a beautiful picture, it's true:

evil is selfishness and nothing else.
