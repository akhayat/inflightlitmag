Continents go slow. The earth’s crust, always changing. Movement on the plates.
Magma seeps up to the ocean floor. Land masses
drift and drift apart. It seems so gradual, a few centimeters a year, but one day
India, floating up from the Arabian peninsula
bumps into Asia and voila!
The Himalayas.

Kelp goes fast. It starts as spores
parachuting to the bottom of the sea, attaching with their holdfasts then
they grow up, two feet a day, up, up two hundred feet
to where the ocean yields to air.

California sea otters, readying for sleep,
turn and turn, hold hands, twine themselves in kelp
so they won’t drift off,
won’t go bumping into Asia in the night.

Between the holdfasts and the magma,
in that underwater, over-lava world
resides the memory of oneness
the yearning there to merge, as South America and Africa were merged,
as we all were merged in one Pangaea,
before the fall.

The plates, they
sigh and shift.
