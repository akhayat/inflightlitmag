The branch snapped off the tree.  Gabby jumped onto the ledge of the building adjacent to it and spread her arms and legs like an eagle.  She closed her eyes and prayed.  Praying always calmed her down.  After a couple of Hail Marys she dared to look; it was almost more than she could take.  She was at least thirty feet off the ground. The bough she had stood on moments before was now hanging off the tree trunk like an oar clinging to a boat.  
Her body shaking, her head spinning, she looked for a way out. Her face was so close to the wall she could discern the mossy smell of the bricks.   In her mind’s eye she saw herself falling down, falling onto the cement path below. She could hear her body splash onto concrete, could see it break into a thousand pieces. Her arms and legs lay twisted like an Aztec god’s, her innards, coiled like a snake, spilled out and mixed with her brain, which, oatmeal-like and gooey, was splattered all over the walkway. It made her nauseous.
Santa María, rece por me. She promised to never do anything this stupid again. Santa María, rece por me. She vowed to never listen to gossip again.  
Good thing Laura’s and Martìn’s apartment was close. She told her heart to stop racing, told her arms to hold on tight, told her legs to take a step. Anything, anything to get away from her predicament. There! She took her first step. Now another one. Excellent! Already the kitchen window, tipped open, was less than an arm’s length away. Only a handful more steps. Terror no longer gripped her so. She could almost feel her devil-may-care attitude coming back. One, two, three, four. She was close enough now to stretch out her arm. She gripped the frame, took one final step, then ducked her head. She beamed when she bobbed up on the other side and looked straight into Laura 's kitchen. She let out a sigh of relief when she saw that everything looked unchanged.  Laura’s tall woven baskets still stood in the corner by the pantry, most likely still filled with clutter.  Her Mapuche runner with its intricate geometric design continued to cover the large wooden table.  She had worried for nothing. Had risked her life for nothing! Everything was unchanged. She should have known Martín better. Should have trusted him.
Then she noticed a peculiar smell.  It was a trace of a perfume she did not recognize. Did rumor have it right for once? Gabby needed to hurry on and check this out.  The only way to do this was to climb onto the balcony, but it was still several steps away. She had little time to spare. As she got closer she could hear Tango music spilling out from the inside. Intimate, sensual, dynamic, playful tango music. Had Martín lost his senses? She was now fueled by anger and indignation and felt more comfortable walking along the ledge. Soon, she reached the balcony, grabbed the railing, climbed over it, jumped onto the concrete floor, and straddled her legs. She felt like she had reached the top of Mount Everest.
But she had no time to celebrate her victory. She walked over where the wicker patio stood, perked her ears, and looked straight ahead. In front of her, separated by a few feet and a thin glass panel, danced the  woman who had stolen Laura's place and, if Gabby were not vigilant, would annihilate her existence.
Nothing about this woman appealed to Gabby. Her body twirled and bent and was unpleasant to look at. Nails hung off her fingers like ripe fruit, the dress was too tight for her athletic figure, and her hair was cut into a bob that accentuated the sharp angles of her vulgar face.  She leashed her hands to Martín’s neck as he glided across the room with ease and elegance. When Martín smiled at the woman, it made Gabby’s stomach churn. She wanted to grab him by his throat and shake him. What was he thinking?
Somewhere, far away, a church clock chimed six times. Martín looked at his wristwatch, said something to the woman, and hurried out of the room. The woman grabbed her purse and ran after him.  Gabby thought she could hear the thump, thump, thump of the woman’s stiletto heels.  Then it was all quiet.  
Gabby remained on the balcony, unsure what to do next.  Suddenly Laura appeared on the inside. She looked much younger, like she had back in Argentina. Her hair was straight, the skin tight, her face glowing. Gabby waved at Laura, but Laura did not seem to see. She needed to get in. Gabby grabbed the door handle and shook it hard. To her astonishment it gave in.  But by the time Gabby had entered, Laura was gone. The apartment was empty. Only the curtain was still swaying.
It was all too much for Gabby.  She needed a break to think this through. Where Laura had stood was now a Champagne bottle and two glasses, one empty, the other half-filled. Gabby suddenly felt thirsty, grabbed the bottle, and filled the empty glass to the brim. She toasted to an invisible Laura before she gulped it down. The Champagne tasted warm and the fizz was all gone, and Gabby puckered her lips.  When she put the glass down she noticed marks of a lipstick on her glass.  She rubbed it off with a scarf, not Laura's, that was sitting on a chair.  Underneath the scarf was an iPhone.  Gabby grabbed the phone on impulse.
The taste of the champagne had scarcely melted off her tongue when Gabby heard the key turn in the door and the stiletto heels return. She rushed to hide behind the curtain.
“I must have put it on the table,” the woman said in a shrill voice. Was everything about her coarse and vulgar?
“Call it,”  Martìn prompted.
Realizing that the phone she clutched in her hand might be ringing at any moment, Gabby, her back against the wall, slithered down, and placed the phone on the floor.  She gave it a jab and rose, knowing she was about to be discovered.
The woman came so close to the curtain, Gabby could smell her perfume.  It was the same mothball smell she had noticed in the bathroom.   Gabby’s heart raced until she could tell by the other’s exclamation of joy that she had found her phone.   
Once more the couple left and peace returned to the apartment, but no matter how often Gabby called out for Laura she did not come back.  Gabby could feel her stomach cramp up. Never before had Laura not answered.  
When Gabby returned to her car, the key was gone.  It had been in the pocket of her sweater and must have fallen out.  Going back to the apartment was out of the question as the door had fallen shut.  She had to call her husband.
