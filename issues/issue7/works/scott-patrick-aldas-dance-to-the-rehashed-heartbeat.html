The music seeps down through your ears and turns in your stomach,
    knotted, boiling, pushing up goose bumps.  

There’s something wrong with the whole scene, waiting for your stuck-up girlfriend
to call it off while your head’s full of something sinister, like in high school,
when you sheepishly implored the TA with the T&A to score you pot that
smelled like skunk and cost 25 dollars per gram.

Since then it’s been go, go, go, buying for every ride that takes you up to the
wholly chemical circus, while your peers dug for admission to Life’s
hokey carnival with jobs and schools and churches and

“I thought they said God was this way.”

And when the TV-baked minds of your generation, who sat cackling in padded
    rooms at their own hackneyed wit, while senile crows munched on
    their egos as black beaks cawed out Iran, Vietnam, Afghanistan, and Uncle
Sam, you chewed old potato chips and meditated to your own white noise as
your veins pulsed.

‘Cause you’re not here to find Jesus in rehabs and madhouses, or find
    yourself in a

Pseudoscientificnewageholistichaze,

But to burn, burn, burn, like a fucking meteor weighed down by cheap kicks,
    trying to see how far your mind will go and knowing you can only see
    the edge with your head turned around 180 degrees as you fall face
    first into the abyss.

And all this as the music releases you, like some dark eyed woman. Her hands loosen
the grip ‘cause your loins are bombed out by a dialysis of cheap tar that has
turned your blood thick and black.

Your last thoughts before your head snaps back, unconscious, are like Kurtz Brando
lying stiff and erect on a bamboo rug as Martin Sheen looks on with terrified
delight:

The horror, the horror.
