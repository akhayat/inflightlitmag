red neon pulses like veins pumping blood.
at my desk the light bounces off a mirror
& hits my scotch glass with a thump.
i take one more sip & end up somewhere
i don't recognise. there has been a murder,
no one i know, but i listen to passing
conversations to gleam a #kernel #of truth.
the police will be here any minute
"wrong place wrong time" & i have
more gleam than truth. i recall a face
flawless as spilt milk & know i have
the right to a phone call. so i try to put
a name to the face,  but the line is down &
they have to let me go. i suspect i know too much
but i'm not writing it down just yet.

blood pumps through veins like red neon flashing
outside my room. snippets of conversation float
through my window like embers ahead of a bush
fire. a voice suggests i should be somewhere else,
another poem maybe, but this is a one poem
town & the sheriff is drunk again.

across the street there is a red neon sign. i'm
walking down beecroft road. this is the suburbs;
there is nothing to see except for cars & houses
& trees. there is a squashed possum on the road,
blood dried on the bitumen near its head
like a final thought bubble
"oh shit."

reaching for the scotch glass i cut my finger
on a chip in the rim. a drop of blood falls
into the single malt (a gift from my corporate brother)
& hangs for an instant in the scotch like a hot air balloon
in a predawn sky. i wonder how the heavy glass
came to be chipped & how it came to be under
the coffee table. i hear possums in the ceiling,
they run along the wires & onto the roof
then find their way in like spies.


they find a body in the car they pull from the river.
my card is in a back pocket. i am called in,
i've been drinking.  
whose face is this?
a memory?
i remember nothing
except blood.
