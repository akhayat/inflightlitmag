The bus stopped suddenly, jolting the passengers forward…and Tamika out of her iPod induced reverie. The doors hissed open, let on some passengers, hissed closed, and continued its route to central London. Tamika settled back into her seat and checked her watch; she was meeting her classmate, Sanae, at half past eight and the slow progress of the bus meant that she was going to be late. A rush of agitation shook her as the impersonal inertia of London transport threatened to further delay the news she felt an urgent need to reveal. She had wanted to tell Sanae the day before, but her classmate had a notoriously bad relationship with Fridays, usually not waking up until early afternoon and missing class completely. So Tamika, feeling that face-to-face was the best way to reveal such juicy gossip, had made do with a non-committal text message about having something she wanted to talk about.
‘It’s okay, it’s okay,’ she reassured herself as the bus’s progress picked up. ‘Sanae is always late anyway.’
Understanding that her previous reverie was forever broken, she tugged her earphones out and gazed out at the scenes of London life now ebbing past the window. She had only been living in London for three months and was still adjusting to the differences from Tokyo, not least the architecture which, with its aura of casual history, never failed to elicit wonder from her.  
The bus terminated near Trafalgar Square; she was meeting Sanae at Piccadilly Circus and decided to walk the remainder of the journey. She approached the National Gallery and conspicuously ascended the steps, turned around and looked at Big Ben burning benignly in the half light of the mild August evening. She closed her eyes, smiled, and savoured the feel of his arms around her, the intoxicating aroma of his aftershave, and the touch of his lips on hers.
‘I can’t believe this,’ she said. ‘You’re my teacher!’
‘Yeah…it’s a little strange,’ he replied, ‘…but it happens a lot’.
‘What?!’
‘I mean in private English language schools!’ he added. ‘What I mean is, it’s not like a state school, is it? Everyone at the school is over eighteen…I mean, you’re what, twenty…?’
‘…five’.
‘Exactly. You’re a mature woman…you can make your own choices, right?’
‘Definitely,’ she answered, breaking into a smile, draping her arms around his neck and leaning in to taste him once more.
She opened her eyes and looked again at the site of her triumph, took a deep breath, and set off to meet Sanae. She floated through the crowds in Leicester Square towards Piccadilly Circus with a slick ease that made her proud. Being a Tokyo girl, she was no stranger to crowds, but the manifested dream of living in London, which she had nurtured since she was a teenager, imbued the streets with a magic the likes of which Tokyo had never inspired in her.  
As she expected, when she arrived at the restaurant, Sanae was nowhere to be seen. A rueful smile played across her lips. She went in and sat at an empty table near the window and a waiter duly came over and offered her a menu which she idly perused. She had been having meals with Sanae on a weekly basis for the past nine weeks. They first met at the small, independent English Language school in Central London where they both studied. Originally signed up for a morning course, Tamika had anticipated late nights in the future and changed her schedule to the afternoon. As she entered, she saw Sanae in the middle of the class and without thinking walked over to the empty seat next to her.
‘…and where do you think you’re going?’ the teacher asked with mock earnestness.
‘Here,’ Tamika replied. ‘Is it okay?’
‘No, it certainly is not okay!’ He replied. ‘Do you know her? Are you friends?’
‘No.’
‘But you’re Japanese, right?’
‘Yes, how did…how did you know?’ She looked at him: he was young, possibly her age…maybe a little bit older. He was smiling and had kind eyes, which reassured her.
‘I know these things…and I know she is Japanese too,’ he gestured to Sanae, who smiled. ‘…I know it’s about 6,000 miles to Japan from here, which is about 9,000 kilometres, and I know you didn’t come all this way to sit next to another Japanese person. What’s your name?’
‘Tamika.’
‘Tamika. Nice name. I’m Lee. It’s a pleasure to meet you Tamika. Look! Sit here! I’ll even warm it up for you!’ He slapped the seat of a chair on the opposite side of the room and rubbed it vigorously. Everyone laughed. Tamika took her new seat, and the class continued.
After the class, Tamika and Sanae nervously introduced themselves and went to the school café where over several coffees they soon found they had much in common: they were roughly the same age, they had been in London for a similar time, and they both had an intermediate level grasp of English, so shared many similar frustrations. They had also both been dreaming of living in London for many years and quickly bonded by sharing what they had experienced so far, and the experiences they desired during their stay. At the end of the week, they went to an Italian restaurant Sanae had read about in a guide listing the best cheap restaurants in London. They were impressed and decided to visit a different restaurant from the guide every week, usually on a Friday after class but occasionally, like tonight, on the Saturday.
The waiter brought over a glass of water. Tamika smiled graciously but left it untouched. She had met Lee on the Thursday evening. She knew which bus-stop he took after finishing work and planned an “accidental” meeting with military precision. She had never done anything similar before, but over the weeks since that first class, her attraction to him had grown to an unmanageable degree; half electrified thrill, half frenzied anxiety. She had to do this. She knew he liked her. She saw how he looked at her in class: during some boring grammar exercise she would often cast him a surreptitious glance to find he was looking at her too, and then they would exchange conspiratorial smiles. She even came to feel that he only gave the class the grammar exercises so they could perform their illicit ritual. Sometimes after school on a Friday, everyone would go to the local pub to socialise, and every time they went she felt like he spoke to her more than anyone else…all this evidence made her sure he had feelings for her. But the school probably didn’t permit teachers to have relationships with students, which she reasoned was why he hadn’t asked her out on a date. But she didn’t need protecting. She was old enough to know what she was doing.
‘Hey Tamika!’
‘Hey Lee!’
‘I didn’t know you got this bus. Have you been shopping?’ He gestured to the small boutique bag she was carrying as a prop.
‘Yes. I bought a necklace from a funky little independent boutique. “Funky”, remember you taught us that? I was exploring…seeing a bit of London. Like you always tell us to do.’ Her heart beat manically: she knew his bus could appear at any moment.
‘Wow,’ he chuckled, ‘you’re such a good student! Are you going home now? I thought you got a different bus.’
‘Actually, I wasn’t waiting for a bus. I just stopped here to think what I want to do now. I was going to have a drink with Sanae, but she has cancelled.’
‘Typical Sanae,’ he laughed, ‘So unreliable!’
‘Yeah! Every time! So I stopped here to think…and met you. It’s a…ah! That word you taught us…a coincidence!’ She flashed him a proud smile which he returned. ‘Actually, Lee, I don’t want to go home yet. Do you know any good pubs near here?’
‘Yeah, loads! But are you gonna drink by yourself?’
‘Yeah. Why not? You said people often do it in England.’
‘I dunno, seems a bit…won’t you get…’
‘Hey! If you’re free, do you want to have a quick drink with me? Oh wait, do you have plans?'
‘No…erm…actually I was just going to go home and probably watch a film. Yeah, alright. Cool, just one though, yeah?’
‘Okay, just one. You know a place?’
‘Yeah, I know a nice one near Trafalgar. Let’s go.’
   
As they dressed the following morning a thousand thoughts crowded Tamika’s mind, each one with an accompanying emotion.
‘It’s so annoying that I have to go to work today,’ he said, massaging his eyes to secure his contact lenses in place. He opened his eyes and looked at her. ‘Because, to be honest, the only thing I really want to do is rip off your clothes and get you back in my bed.’
She smiled. ‘Yeah, I want you to do that.'
He paced across the room to her and firmly took her by the waist. She braced herself to be taken again but he smiled his soft smile, released his grip, and instead pushed her hair behind her ears and planted a gentle kiss on her top lip.
‘I would if I could. But I really have to work…I’ve phoned in sick enough times already this year!’
‘Are you free tonight?’
‘Ah! Actually no…I said I was going to meet a friend tonight! Sorry!’
‘It’s okay. We didn’t know we would meet yesterday and…this…would happen. So if you have plans tonight it’s okay. I understand.’
‘What about Saturday?’
‘Ah…I want to, but I will meet Sanae. Every week we go to a restaurant from her guidebook.'
‘Oh yeah. I remember you told me about it before. Ermm...’ His hesitation lasted only a fraction of a second, but Tamika knew exactly what he was going to say. ‘Are you going to…tell anyone…about…us?’
‘What? You mean tell Sanae?’
‘Well…anyone really. But Sanae especially. I think she loves to gossip.’
Tamika laughed. ‘Not really. She’s the same as most girls, I think. But it’s no problem. I understand. It will be difficult for you if students gossip, won't it?’
‘To be honest, yes. I mean, I’m not ashamed of last night at all…but it would make work more awkward.’
‘Okay. Let’s promise to keep the whole thing secret: I won’t tell any students and you won’t tell any teachers.’
Lee laughed. ‘Of course not!’
‘Great! So promise…’ She held her right fist up with her little finger extended.
‘Ah! A Japanese promise.’ He smiled, duplicated the gesture and hooked his little finger around hers.

The door burst open and in spilled Sanae in a rapid flurry of formal Japanese apologies. Tamika smiled. ‘It’s okay. Everyone knows you’re always late.’
‘That’s not true!’ Sanae remonstrated, whipping off her coat and taking her seat. ‘You’ll never…’
‘Don’t be offended. It’s part of your charm.’ Tamika interrupted. In Sanae’s company she seemed oddly cured of the impatient anxiety she had felt all evening, and calmly, but quickly, decided that she would wait until the food had arrived before telling Sanae her news. In light of the promise she had made with Lee, she had deliberated all through the last two days if she should tell Sanae or not…but she had to tell someone and she felt that if she made Sanae promise to absolutely not tell anyone, then she could trust her. ‘I haven’t ordered yet but the menu looks…’
‘Sorry to interrupt…and I know you’ve got something you want to tell me, hopefully about a guy…but I’ve got some of my own which I just have to tell you and I can’t wait! You’ll never guess who I spent the night with? Oh God! We made a promise not to tell anyone, so I’m trusting you to really not tell anyone. Deal?’
Tamika looked at Sanae’s eyes flashing with innocent, enthusiastic triumph as her extended little finger quivered nakedly across the table in anticipation of a promise to be made.
