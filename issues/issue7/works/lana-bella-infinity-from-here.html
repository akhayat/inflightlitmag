Waiting on a girl with sun-
drenched skin, 
mildew-colored hair,
slim brown legs moved like
alabaster from the grotto
of nymphs,
and whose lips led a slew of 
ancient war and ghosts 
eroded into
her poisoned hours.
I pictured the sinkage behind
her thatch-roofed house,
a distant way beyond 
the town's well and always 
veiled in ply of muck and
splintered bones, 
was where she had said
she could disappear
down forever,
huddled close to the ground-
ward carbon and came to 
be the roots for worms,
lied in the forgotten grasp 
of language as
the loam remedies her 
ethereal cracks,
oblivious to the stir of time.
