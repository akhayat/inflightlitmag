1.

Lemon drops melt like troubles down my cancer ravaged throat. I thought I was a made man, with my black leather hat and my Cuban cigars, but I am unmade. I have unmade myself. That’s always the way it is, whether you’ve organized your crimes or have left them unorganized, whether you’re Julius Caesar or just some douche bag out on the dirty street corner trying to sing a cappella.

2.

I’m standing next to a tree that’s like the one that stood in our yard in front of the farmhouse as I was growing up, a Black Walnut. I don’t think this is a Black Walnut, though it spreads out in the same muscular way. It’s hard for me to know. My vision is blurry. I’ve been shot. I’m leaning against this tree, my last intimate relationship, my last significant other. This tree. I’ll call it Black Walnut.

And I’m wondering why humans lost their hair, why we didn’t keep the luxuriant hair of the orangutan or the thick coat of a mountain gorilla. At least I’d be warm, out here in the snow.
