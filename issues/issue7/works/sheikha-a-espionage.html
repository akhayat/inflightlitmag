The days of starvation are here again;
it’s the last day today and the moon
quickly slides behind the skimpy
branches from my hunger-gaze.
I can’t help but eye like the blind
wolf that smells its meat without
knowing how to reach it; the brown
has descended and my eyes lose
their colours. I have been standing in
bright lights, gazing at the white neons
because I thought they’d return
the sparkling glaze in them. But all
I see now are wrinkly frazzled shades
of pink, the signs of
fading,
fazing,
falling,
the signs of shadows on tendered
concretes on a bed of red sunflowers.
