My heart gushes crap
when it's not throwing up.
It's tobacco stained.
And smells of swamp water.
In the seclusion of my room,
I pick at it like a nostril.
When I'm out and about,
I pretend it's my mouth.

My heart is as sarcastic
as the smartest guy in the room.
Except it knows too much
for its own good.
It can quote to you
from the book of atria
or the epistle according
to pulmonary circulation.
But when it comes to love,
it whips itself hard and raw
to make the only point it can.

My heart's been stolen once or twice
and then dumped on my doorstep.
It's been drunk down
and pissed off a penthouse rail.
It was used as a football
and a baton in a relay race.
It bleeds.
It oozes puss.
It's not the one I asked for
but, then again,
when did I ever ask for one?
