Oh, Whitman, my brother! Where are you now?

Life is cold and rotten.
There is no center.
Desperation is nature, a vile desire to live against the unceasing tides of death.
It comes at me from every angle,
It laughs in my face through the light and the dark.
The stars glimmer -
Then die.

Oh, Whitman, my brother! Where are you now?
Now that death has usurped your form
Where are your atoms? Where lies the dust that was your skin?
Where now the electric pulse that was your passion?

Stillness is a fiction.
Mobility is function and runs to the ceasing,
The cleaver,
The scythe to scalp the hearts of love and dreams.
Life runs a marathon until it runs out,
Runs still in the ne'er ending thrust of death and desolation,
And I run breathless in its grip,
Surrounding by the tortured phantoms that yet breathe because they cannot stand still.

Oh, Whitman, my brother! Where are you now?
Your words run the pages and ring in my head,
And my life is the breath of the soon to be dead.
Soon my dust will join yours and the billions,
Atoms pulsing deathless, yet not living.
I scream for the escape but find in it the prison of merciless life.

Suicide is infinitely deathless, impossibly vain.

Oh, Whitman, my brother! Our fate has always been sealed,
We grieve and we dream to no possible avail,
A freedom and curse to be yet borne on wings of the unborn.
