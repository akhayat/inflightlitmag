(for Ray Bradbury)

These nights, the cats drag autumn back to me like freshly caught prey.
Tiny burrs nestle amongst thick-fingered fur:
Their bodies wriggle as I seek to lift and pick and clean.
They carry the scent of night along with them;
Of careful movements through waning hedges,
Thick with the darkly clustered final fruits of autumn,
Sweet with grass once warmed by the clear-eyed sun.
There is a wildness in them, now, that I have not seen before;
Eyes look above me to the distance -
No longer fixed to mine for attention, approbation -
Nostrils flared in a heady moment for the scent of prey.
They stop - briefly - for moments of love that they still remember,
Then glide, silent, beneath stars that will coldly guide their way.
