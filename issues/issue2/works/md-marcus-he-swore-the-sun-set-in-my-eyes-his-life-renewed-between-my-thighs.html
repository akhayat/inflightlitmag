Falsehood of lovers
awestruck dumb in reverence
to my broken heart
which he will mend if I’m a good girl
move a little slower next time
keep my mouth from going so wide
and stop scaring him
with all my silly violence

Catching beads of sweat
collecting in the small of my back
in the faint hairs
found between my breasts
no matter, a tongue cannot sense
such delicate imperfections
must not worry over perfection, anyway
must not, must not, must not

and all this to say he loves me—ha!

Modesty and indiscretion
publically collide
an emotional melee
for territory, title,
and forthcoming reputations to uphold
but it was all just a show anyway
and I miss you and think about you
more often than I should, more often
than infatuation and lust
ever deserved
