[After Maurice Ravel’s Une Barque sur l’Ocean]


Ingenue and me, we
took the boat out on the open bay.
It threatened rain all morning,
but cleared as we got on the water,
incremental oar power
rocked by wakes of the jet-set’s cabin cruisers,
most of those external compensations
are captained by business warriors
who bought boats as a discount route
to the hearts and thighs of potential trophies.
We climb and dip the minor swells,
Ingenue shines as the sun breaks out.
We’re feelin’ copacetic, slowed to reflect
surrounding elements. The flicker
isn’t lost on us. This rowboat’s just our speed.
