It was cold, you said –


It was cold

when your mother read out the Little Match Girl to you

and you shed your first tear.


It was cold

the night your mother lay motionless in a pool of red.


It was cold

the day your father was knocked dead in the wrestling bout.


It was cold

when you first stepped out of the house alone

trembling in the tattered shoes on your 14-year-old feet.


It was cold

when the man lifted your skirt and made you bleed.


It was cold

when they took away the house.


It was cold

when I first met you outside the café you worked at.


It was cold

when I asked you to marry me,

and you said no.
