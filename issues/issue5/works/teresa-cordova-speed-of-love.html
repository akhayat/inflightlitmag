There was a time when misery did
not love company:
when you married in your
years of teenage wasteland.
Decisions made on your feet.
No full frontal lobe to guide you yet—
only cloud nine to keep you afloat,
sane.
A disposable camera at hand to keep
ancient record of
the smiles.
You both wore them then like tattoos.

Neon colored love!
That was the style, all the rage.
Every step you took
there it was,
all the leftovers
in your pockets.
It was so bright
A revolution!
A blind man would eventually see it. Even from a distance—
love could see you too.

Today it would only take
Three times ten to the eighth power
meters per second
To realize how long it takes for love
to orbit you.
It is
already gone.
