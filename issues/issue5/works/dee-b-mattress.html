Overlaid and flat
and asleep while the sun holds your ears. When you close
your eyes do you see any signs of life. I came as a
twin sized bed
that never needed trading up. I’m fine but bloated. Labeled but uncategorized. I’m finite, and
that’s somehow terrifying and comforting at times

to know that I’ll be tossed to the alley, maybe used as some shelter
for a bright, swollen mouse or a hobo
or maybe an empty box of Sno-Caps or something
else that’s gone and alright.

But if I somehow can be “alright” what comes
next for a mattress? What gets a hold to pull me
to the nearest pile to burn, or squashed and get piled underneath at the landfill,
or set out to sea on some barge to soak into depths and brush nothing on the
way down?

I’d rather stay awhile. Maybe catch your imprint, maybe there’s still time
to feel brand new
or used as some petite
thing’s novelty.
