…but that window has closed.
Only fossils remain from that era ending
in a painless parting,
but the remnants of youth are scattered on the floor
in heaps and strewn in a careless urgency.
The figurines we once cherished
accumulate more dust than value,
memories decompose in plain sight.
She sought to embalm our childhood,
letting linger recollections
while silently watching
clothes grow old, outworn
and half forgotten.

The necromancer couldn’t let our spirits rest.
In her head, we never left.
She conjures together a timeless life
from dead air trapped inside
breathless walls.
The floorboards heave as the foundation
whispers what it has seen.
She dreams of those missing pieces
that render inner peace obsolete,
repeating and repeating and repeating
that she doesn’t need to be reminded
that time is passing her by.
We won’t let her forget –
we are the haunters that lurk
between two dissociated worlds.

Our passion ignites as we dance
around the points of flame
hurled from the curl of our vicious tongues.
Throwing fire like the snowballs
we never rolled in Southern California heat,
constantly jumping through burning
hoops of expectation just to fall into a pile
of ashes and smoldering cinders, to no avail.
For the sake of transparency,
there must be a veil
forever keeping our two worlds apart.

Behind the wall of sleep
we meet where parallels collide;
but in this waking life, we senselessly
repeat the same mistakes and possess
the nerve to place all blame
on some unalterable scheme of fate.
Whether separated by oceans, zip-
codes, or some commotion sparked
by the revival of a fight from 2003:
we just can’t let the ghosts stay dead.

Brother, we were given life to share
and love to care for one another.
Instead, we standby watching it all subside
and silently dwindle into a passing waft of smoke,
exhaled without care or attention.
You breathe toxicity, secrete meanness,
a bitter coating to disguise inner weakness.
Nothing will save this house,
and I doubt you’ll ever leave –
a slave to impulses instinctively obeyed,
a perpetually enraged enemy of reason.
When did love become a treasonous act,
or need a reason to pervade?

The walls are stained and engrained
with a sadness unparalleled.
Windows paved over to prevent
the pestilence from spreading.
Gates welded shut, caked
with rust from years of decay.
The door to your room was dead-bolted
to keep contained the sacred contents –
a product of our mutual maker,
a plague you weren’t able to escape.
You choose to be entombed in that crypt
all for the sake of proving the merit
of your own hollow, unfollowed words.

It was never meant to be this way,  
but things won’t change when you refuse
to even try seeing a broader point of view
because your heart is petrified from lack of use.
There’s no use fighting and yelling at a wall.
It was never meant to be this way,
but we let it rot before our eyes
and must both bear the stench of regret
billowing from the inside, out.
