Listen to my whisper: if I should ever make a 1970’s face while we make love, while we are making current-day love, while we are making contemporary love, I will completely understand if you immediately decide to end things between us, even if means that you decide to end things right there in mid-midnight coitus, on our ruby-red silken sweat-bed, on our body-fluid waterbed, on our black jiggly raft, afloat on the splorchy, wet dreams of our loinhawks. Even it means that you end me. If I should ever grit my throat or groan my teeth or grimace in the way that the despicable, turquoise and greasy people of the 1970’s did, you will have the natural right to kill me until I die to death in our marriage room. We both have studied history and we have learned from it; we know that all of the people of the 1970’s, without exception, were pornographic people. And that they were greasy also. So greasy, the word should be spelled with two e’s, a z and a y: greezy. We both know that the shiny, polyester people of the 1970’s made porn pancakes when they made pancakes, and that they made porn castles when they made sand castles and that they made porn love when they made love, that they exchanged their body greases freely, and that sequins popped off of them when they reproduced and made babies that reeked of Coppertone and mustache wax, and there is no way to go back in time to stop them; there is no way to go back in hope to stop them; there is no way to go back in love to stop them. In all of my purple life, I have tried, and succeeded, in my own small and God-blessed way, to prevent my hair from ever looking 1970’s. Dreams are made from the woody, brown-stained hope chest in the chest, from inside of us. Dreams don’t come from the 1970’s, those aquamarine and gaudy years. Nightmares come from there, and we won’t lovemake in the mean, uncouth dark of such a Jordache place.
