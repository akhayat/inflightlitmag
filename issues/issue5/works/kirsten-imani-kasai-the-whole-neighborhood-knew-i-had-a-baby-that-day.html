Sweet child!
I add my breath to your breath
inhale and sing, chant, howl
through each contraction
my long deep “ohhhhs”
fill our house with
sound, float out the
windows and over the
fence into the ears
of the little boy
next door.
I birth you on waves of my breath.
Open your mouth, the midwives
say, make your throat a
tunnel, your mouth a
cave, each end of you
is connected. Both
mouths must open wide.
My breath travels through my
blood, the extension cord
connecting us transmutes my electric
charge to you, and when you
emerge to take your first
breath, it is my song
you pull inside
my chant, my howl.
Sweet child, I add my breath
to your breath.
