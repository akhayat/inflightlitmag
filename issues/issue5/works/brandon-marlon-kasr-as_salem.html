Somewhere a castle…though I pursue it fruitlessly,
searching for moat and turret,
seeking directions in secondhand Arabic
from the kasbah of Fez to the souk in Fostat.

To my chagrin, I am solitary in my quest:
none have ever heard tell of it,
knowing neither its legendary splendor
nor its monumental gate or iron portcullis.

Although keen to dust wandering from callused feet,
they urge me to retreat from so-called illusion,
as if I am prematurely senile or quasi-quixotic,
a credulous nomad vainly adventuring for treasures.

In Bagdad they openly mock my mirage;
in Marrakesh they giggle behind dyed veils;
in Cairo the mob pelts me with vitriol and vegetables;
and in Ramallah I’m welcomed with the customary stone shower.

Never was an address so esoteric,
yet I remain with unsatisfied soul,
wandering wildernesses, panting after peace.
Somewhere, a castle…Insha’Allah, I’ll stumble upon it in my lifetime.




1. Arabic: Palace of Peace
