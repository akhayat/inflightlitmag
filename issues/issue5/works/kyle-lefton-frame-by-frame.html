One too many journalists were crucified
on newsstands, hung
and displayed in plain
sight, whose names were red-listed
in the fine print each dismal morning
on the blackened Astroturf
of new-age Salem marketplaces.
An excess of disarray
and madness characterized the era;
immortalizing us for the major breakthroughs
of knowledge – making first contact
with the Technicolor god.
The subsequent breakdowns
of common sense have been waging
war ever since.

My life clung desperately to a strict
and narrow path, seeking the few meager
eases and first world comforts
awarded to an old world immigrant.
I wasn’t hopping freights,
or waiting for fate to pick me up
on forgotten back roads
that lead to Nowhere, Ohio
or Exactly Where I Need To Be, USA
like good ‘ol Jack,
and Howling strange
manifestos at the wailing wall
in Bronx. I drove
downstream on a one-way
floodplain as the others drowned
swimming against the vicious
currents of their irreverent vicissitudes

I went along with life
as it came and went to be.
I hear you, Bobby D,
the times, indeed, are a’ changing,
but not me, not me.
I’m still here, just waiting out
in this shack of a house,
in this renovated den resembling
a hospital room
with adjustable bed and a television
set that never blinks.

It stares back watching me.
It holds my gaze knowing
that I stood idle as windows
were shattered in Little Rock,
spurned from those who challenged
and occupied segregated High Schools.
It knew I skimmed the news
of Hungarian students scattering
from showers of bullets pitter-
pattering into flesh that hungered
for revolution, eyes dripping
from teargas fumes and blood billowing
into a unified coat of arms.
The dead, wrapped in Red flags
were hoisted upwards under the shadow
of Russia’s King Koba, ripped
from the tapestry of life
like silent red stars that gleamed
a patriotic hate for all those who stood
defiantly in opposition.

I sat back and watched Sputnik puncture
the outer fringes of atmosphere,
probing the skies in the name of conquest
from one Mother to a much older one.
The space-race started when we discovered
atoms could be split and used to disassemble
brutish regimes and obedient molecular structures
of the virus sweeping across Europe’s axis.  
My mother was ripped away at a young age.
The Universe received my tragedy indifferently
as I watched the nucleus of my world decay
and ricochet into stray fragments
of a divorced nuclear family.
And just like that, a nebula
formed on a teardrop;
I can’t hug Her ghostly spiral arms
when they are cradling a zodiac of warriors,
mystical beasts and gods
within a spectral manger of constellations.
Yet, my spine shivers when I stare
at these skies that witness eternity.
I find comfort knowing
that through an extended
transformation of carbon,
every explosion has its purpose.

Youth passed by in a flash-
back that wasn’t even mine,
yet I never asked who is dreaming me,
content without knowing
if there is a deeper meaning
to these fleeting memories that seemingly
have a life of their own.
My life has become a hologram,
no matter which way the light catches
the contents, the same sadness flickers
from the depths of a personal oblivion.
I haunt these halls as an echo
of the cosmic pulse, my remnants a throbbing
legacy in the veins of a single man’s
vanity and silent, accepted insanity.
